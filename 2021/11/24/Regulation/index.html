1 Introduction & Environment

There is a monopolistic firm and a mass one of consumers.

Inverse-demand function:

​ V:[0,1]→[0,vˉ]V:[0,1] \rightarrow[0, \bar{v}]V:[0,1]→[0,vˉ]

​ A quantity-price pair (q,p)∈[0,1]×[0,vˉ](q, p) \in [0,1] \times[0, \bar{v}](q,p)∈[0,1]×[0,vˉ] is feasible if and only if it is below the inverse-demand function, i.e., p⩽V(q)p \leqslant V(q)p⩽V(q). ​ The total value to consumers of quantity qqq is the area under the inverse-demand function given by ∫0qV(z)dz\int_{0}^{q} V(z) \mathrm{d} z∫0q​V(z)dz

Distortion of total surplus(with quantity q):

DSTR⁡(V,C,q)=OPT⁡(V,C)−(∫0qV(z)dz−C(q))\operatorname{DSTR}(V, C, q)=\operatorname{OPT}(V, C)-\left(\int_{0}^{q} V(z) \mathrm{d} z-C(q)\right) DSTR(V,C,q)=OPT(V,C)−(∫0q​V(z)dz−C(q))

Regulatory policies:

​ Revenue of the firm: ρ(q,p)\rho(q, p)ρ(q,p)

**Consumer surplus and Firm payoff : **

CS(V,ρ,q,p)=∫0qV(z)dz−ρ(q,p)FP(V,C,ρ,q,p)=ρ(q,p)−C(q)\mathrm{CS}(V, \rho, q, p)=\int_{0}^{q} V(z) \mathrm{d} z-\rho(q, p)\\ \mathrm{FP}(V, C, \rho, q, p)=\rho(q, p)-C(q) CS(V,ρ,q,p)=∫0q​V(z)dz−ρ(q,p)FP(V,C,ρ,q,p)=ρ(q,p)−C(q)
